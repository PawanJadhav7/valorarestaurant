# Valora AI Investor Metrics

## Overview

This document defines the core SaaS metrics used to measure the growth, efficiency, and financial performance of the Valora AI platform.

These metrics are commonly used by venture capital firms, private equity investors, and SaaS operators to evaluate software businesses.

---

# Core SaaS Metrics

## Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR)

**Definition**

Total recurring revenue generated from subscriptions on an annual basis.

**Formula**

ARR = Monthly Recurring Revenue × 12

Example

MRR = $25,000

ARR = $300,000

---

## Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)

**Definition**

Total subscription revenue generated each month.

**Formula**

MRR = Sum of all active subscriptions

Example

| Customers | Plan | Monthly Revenue |
|------|------|------|
10 | Starter ($199) | $1,990 |
20 | Growth ($499) | $9,980 |
5 | Enterprise ($1500 avg) | $7,500 |

Total MRR = $19,470

---

## Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

**Definition**

Average cost to acquire a new paying customer.

**Formula**

CAC = Total Sales & Marketing Cost / New Customers Acquired

Example

Sales spend = $50,000  
Customers acquired = 100  

CAC = $500

---

## Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)

**Definition**

Total revenue expected from a customer over their lifetime.

**Formula**

LTV = ARPU × Customer Lifetime

Where:

ARPU = Average Revenue Per User

Customer Lifetime = 1 / Churn Rate

Example

ARPU = $499/month  
Churn = 5%

Lifetime = 20 months

LTV = $9,980

---

## LTV / CAC Ratio

**Definition**

Measures efficiency of customer acquisition.

**Formula**

LTV / CAC

Benchmark:

| Ratio | Meaning |
|------|------|
< 1 | Losing money |
1–3 | Acceptable |
3–5 | Strong SaaS business |
> 5 | Extremely efficient |

Target for Valora:

3–5

---

## Gross Revenue Retention (GRR)

**Definition**

Revenue retained from existing customers excluding expansion revenue.

**Formula**

GRR = (Starting Revenue − Churn − Downgrades) / Starting Revenue

Target:

85–95%

---

## Net Revenue Retention (NRR)

**Definition**

Revenue retained from customers including expansion revenue.

**Formula**

NRR = (Starting Revenue − Churn + Expansion) / Starting Revenue

Benchmark:

| NRR | Meaning |
|------|------|
<100% | Revenue shrinking |
100% | Flat |
110–120% | Healthy SaaS growth |
>120% | Elite SaaS performance |

Target for Valora:

110–120%

---

## Churn Rate

**Definition**

Percentage of customers cancelling subscriptions.

**Formula**

Churn = Customers Lost / Total Customers

Benchmark

| Annual Churn | Quality |
|------|------|
<5% | Excellent |
5–10% | Healthy |
>10% | Risky |

Target for Valora:

< 8%

---

# Unit Economics

## Gross Margin

**Definition**

Revenue remaining after infrastructure and support costs.

**Formula**

Gross Margin = (Revenue − Cost of Service) / Revenue

Typical SaaS benchmark:

70–85%

Target for Valora:

75–85%

---

## Payback Period

**Definition**

Time required to recover CAC.

**Formula**

Payback Period = CAC / Monthly Gross Profit per Customer

Target:

< 12 months

# Why These Metrics Matter

Investors evaluate SaaS companies primarily through these indicators.

Healthy SaaS companies demonstrate:

• strong ARR growth  
• efficient customer acquisition  
• low churn  
• high revenue retention  
• scalable unit economics

Valora aims to build a **capital-efficient AI SaaS platform serving the global restaurant industry.**
