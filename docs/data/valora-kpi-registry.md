# Valora AI KPI Registry

## Overview

This document defines all Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) used across the Valora AI platform.

The KPI registry ensures:

- every KPI has a consistent definition
- dashboards and APIs use identical formulas
- ML models rely on standardized features
- financial metrics remain auditable and traceable

Primary analytical data source:

`restaurant.f_location_daily_features`

All KPIs displayed in Valora dashboards originate from this standardized model.

---

# Financial KPIs

## Revenue

**Definition**

Total sales generated by a restaurant location during a given period.

**Formula**

Revenue = SUM(order_total)

**Source**

`restaurant.fact_order`

---

## COGS (Cost of Goods Sold)

**Definition**

Total cost of ingredients used to produce sold menu items.

**Formula**

COGS = SUM(menu_item_cost × quantity_sold)

**Source**

`restaurant.fact_order_item`

---

## Gross Profit

**Definition**

Revenue remaining after food costs.

**Formula**

Gross Profit = Revenue − COGS

---

## Gross Margin %

**Definition**

Percentage of revenue retained after food costs.

**Formula**

Gross Margin % = Gross Profit / Revenue

---

# Cost KPIs

## Food Cost %

**Definition**

Percentage of revenue spent on food ingredients.

**Formula**

Food Cost % = COGS / Revenue

**Industry Benchmark**

Target: 28–35%

---

## Labor Cost %

**Definition**

Percentage of revenue spent on staff wages.

**Formula**

Labor Cost % = Labor Cost / Revenue

**Industry Benchmark**

Target: 25–30%

---

## Prime Cost

**Definition**

Combined cost of food and labor.

**Formula**

Prime Cost = COGS + Labor

---

## Prime Cost %

**Definition**

Percentage of revenue consumed by prime costs.

**Formula**

Prime Cost % = (COGS + Labor) / Revenue

**Industry Benchmark**

Target < 60%

---

# Profitability KPIs

## Contribution Margin

**Definition**

Revenue remaining after variable operating costs.

**Formula**

Contribution Margin = Revenue − Variable Costs

---

## Contribution Margin %

**Formula**

Contribution Margin % = Contribution Margin / Revenue

---

## EBITDA

**Definition**

Earnings before interest, tax, depreciation, and amortization.

**Formula**

EBITDA = Revenue − Operating Expenses

---

## EBITDA Margin %

**Formula**

EBITDA Margin % = EBITDA / Revenue

---

## Break-Even Ratio

**Definition**

Measures how close the business is to covering operating costs.

**Formula**

Break-Even Ratio = Fixed Costs / Contribution Margin

---

# Sales KPIs

## Orders

**Definition**

Total completed orders during the selected time period.

---

## Customers

**Definition**

Total number of unique customers served.

---

## Average Order Value (AOV)

**Definition**

Average revenue generated per order.

**Formula**

AOV = Revenue / Orders

---

## Revenue Per Customer

**Formula**

Revenue per Customer = Revenue / Customers

---

# Operational KPIs

## Sales Per Labor Hour

**Definition**

Measures employee productivity.

**Formula**

Sales per Labor Hour = Revenue / Labor Hours

---

## Table Turnover Rate

**Definition**

Number of times a table is used during a service period.

---

## Service Time

**Definition**

Average time between order placement and food delivery.

---

# Inventory KPIs

## Inventory Turnover

**Definition**

Measures how quickly inventory is used.

**Formula**

Inventory Turnover = COGS / Average Inventory

---

## Days Inventory Outstanding (DIO)

**Definition**

Average number of days inventory remains in stock.

**Formula**

DIO = (Average Inventory / COGS) × 365

---

## Waste %

**Definition**

Percentage of inventory lost due to spoilage or operational inefficiencies.

**Formula**

Waste % = Waste Value / Inventory Value

---

## Stockout Count

**Definition**

Number of times a menu item was unavailable due to lack of inventory.

---

# Cash Flow KPIs

## Accounts Receivable Days

**Formula**

AR Days = (Accounts Receivable / Revenue) × 365

---

## Accounts Payable Days

**Formula**

AP Days = (Accounts Payable / COGS) × 365

---

## Cash Conversion Cycle

**Formula**

Cash Conversion Cycle = DIO + AR Days − AP Days
